    Facts about Anxiety

    • Anxiety disorders are the most common mental illness in the U.S., affecting 40 million adults in the United States age 18 and older
    • Anxiety disorders develop from a complex set of risk factors, including genetics, brain chemistry, personality, and life events.
    • Anxiety disorders are highly treatable, yet only 36% of those suffering receive treatment

    Different Types of Anxiety

    Generalized Anxiety Disorder

    This type of anxiety is characterized by persistent and excessive worry about a variety of different things. People with GAD are always feeling tense and worried, even when there is nothing to feel anxious about. It affects 6.8 million or 3.1% in the US population. The risk for GAD is the highest for children and middle aged adults. Women are twice as likely to be affected. The thought of getting through the day can create anxiety for a person affected by GAD. They dn't know how to stop worrying and feel that it is beyond their control. Many poeple think worry prevents bad things from happening so they believe it is risky to give up worrying.

    Social Anxiety Disorder

    Social Anxiety or social phobia is defined when someone has an intense anxiety or fear of being judged, evaluated negatively, or being rejected in a social situation. People with SAD are scared of people judging then to be stupid, awkward, or boring. Many people with social anxiety disorder also experience strong physical symptoms, such as a rapid heart rate, nausea, and sweating, and may experience full-blown attacks when confronting a feared situation. They are worried about being negatively viewed by others, which can cause them to actively avoid everyday social situations. This makes then feel very lonely and depressed.

    Panic Disorder

    Panic disorder is diagnosed when people experiance unexpcted panic attacks and are constantly worrying about the fear of a recurring attack. Panic disorder usually begans in adulthood (after age 20). Somtimes children could have panic disorder and many experience panic like symptoms. Panic disorder is twice mor common in omen than men. This disorder can interfere with peoples daily lives causing them to miss work, go to many doctors visits, and the may skip out on situations where the fear they might have a panic attack.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What causes anxiety disorders? Researchers have found that axiety disorders run in families just like allergies, diabetes, heart diesease, and many other disorders. axiety disorders usually develop from a complex set of risk factors including brain chemistry, genetics, personality, and life events.

    What treatment options are available? Treatents options for anxiety disorders vary depending on the person and their needs and preferences. They most likely will include either medication or therapy or a combination of both.

    When does anxiety become a disorder? Anxiety is a key motivational tool that helps us focus on important tasks. However, anxiety becomes a problem when it bcomes more intense and lasts longer than typically expected. Also when it starts to cause impairment in social environments such as school and work. Lastly, it is a probem when daily activities are avoided to try and lessen the anxiety.

    What is the best way to overcome an anxiety disoder? The main things to help overcome an anxiety disorder are proper self-help information, understanding and knowing more about your disorder, and getting help form therapists.
